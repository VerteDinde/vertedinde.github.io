About

Keeley Hammond is a software engineer at Think Shift. We’re a friendly little international group of creatives who love shipping large projects, designing award-winning ad campaigns and making your employees love coming to work. You should give us a shout.

Previously, she has served on the leadership team for the Portland chapter of Women Who Code, where she focused on creating and teaching programming curriculum for beginning and advanced levels, helping women begin contributing to OSS. She enjoys speaking at conferences, baking, long-distance running, and hacking away on interesting problems. Keeley primarily works with JavaScript, Node, Angular, Ember, and React. She has strong opinions about clean code, accessibility, testing, and static websites.

Keeley is currently living in Portland, Oregon. By day, she’s extremely interested in front-end development, leading community workshops for Women Who Code, and creating compelling design work. By night, she enjoys running marathons through the mountains of the Columbia Gorge, experimenting and learning new ways to route Internet traffic, and listening to podcasts about American history.
